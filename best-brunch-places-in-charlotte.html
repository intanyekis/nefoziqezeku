Best brunch places in Charlotte WSOC TV

May 2024 · 3 minute read

The best places for brunch in Charlotte have everything you need to make a great addition to college weekends, family celebrations and lazy Sundays. These five best brunches in Charlotte offer boozy fun, tasty traditional or fusion fare and lots of atmosphere.

Boudreax's
Brunch Saturday and Sunday, 11 a.m.-3 p.m.
501 E. 36th St., Charlotte 
704-331-9898
boudreauxsnoda.com

This Cajun joint in the Charlotte arts district serves brunch with flair and is a favorite of Charlotte magazine. Beignets are a must, and where else would brunch involve a croissant Florentine or crab omelet? It's also a great spot to indulge in chicken and waffles or biscuits and gravy.

300 East
Brunch Sundays 10 a.m.-3 p.m.
300 East Blvd., Charlotte 
704-332-6507
300east.net

When you wake up on a weekend morning in Charlotte and want a wholesome, sustainably-sourced, homestyle brunch served in the height of Southern charm, turn to 300 East. A popular starter is baked marinated Vermont goat cheese, and all the eggs are free range and sourced from local family farms. These ingredients are used to advantage in dishes from breakfast pizza to vegetable Benedict. Sides are too numerous to mention, but look for the Wild Turkey Farms Chorizo and Heritage Farms bacon. The wine list is extensive enough to play around with pairings, too.

Cafe Monte
Full breakfast and lunch menu served Sunday from 9 a.m.-3 p.m.
6700 Fairview Road, Charlotte
704-552-1116
cafemonte.net

Say "Oui, oui" to one of the best places for brunch in Charlotte at this French bakery and bistro. Classic French country favorites in the brunch mix include crepes, omelets and fresh pastries. For an exquisite Parisian taste, order the Croque Madame, a ham and Gruyère sandwich topped with a fried egg. 

Tupelo Honey Cafe
Brunch Saturday and Sunday from opening to 4 p.m.
820 South Blvd., Charlotte 
980-225-9175
tupelohoneycafe.com/location/charlotte

Brunch with bloody Marys or mimosas is a fine way to enjoy this cafe dedicated to Southern food that's good for the soul. Made from scratch with fresh ingredients, some of the "don't-miss" items on the brunch menu include fried green tomatoes with goat's cheese grits, a fried chicken BLT and sweet potato pancakes. Add some tasty greens to your weekend menu, too, with Tupelo's signature salad of kale, watermelon radish and quinoa with yogurt dressing.

Eddie's Place
Breakfast served all the time, starting at 8 a.m. on Saturdays and Sundays
617 S. Sharon Amity Road, Charlotte
704-442-0147
eddiesplacerestaurant.com

Beloved by The Culture Trip blog, Eddie's Place serves breakfast all day, including a walloping helping of country fried breakfast, steak and eggs or big fluffy pancakes for a brunch appetite. The waffle in the chicken and waffle dish is fresh-baked and topped with a pecan fried chicken breast and honey cinnamon butter, if you want to delve into this swell brunch tradition in Charlotte.
