It's been a hot minute since we last caught up with Scott McGlynn - so what's he been up to, over the past few months?

We sat down with the influencer, talk show host and Bloody Mary Returns actor to hear about Season 3 of Celebrity Skin Talk.

Plus, he shares what he's excited about, in the skincare world - and the things he'd advise fans to avoid if they're hoping to achieve their best skin yet.

It's Been A Busy Year For Scott McGlynn

Just four months in to 2023, Scott McGlynn has had a bumper start to the year.

For starters, he beams, Bloody Mary Returns has been released - "And it's having a great response."

"I also just wrapped a fun talk series with a men's makeup and skincare brand, which was an amazing opportunity," he adds.

Did we mention that he has several more gigs in the pipeline?

Not to worry, though - a slew of additional gigs doesn't mean he plans on putting his passion project on the back burner.

Au contraire, the opposite is true.

"I've just gone into my third series of my Instagram talk show, Celebrity Skin Talk - and it's incredible! We're still going strong."

What To Expect From The New Season Of "Celebrity Skin Talks"

So, Celebrity Skin Talk is back - but what can fans expect, this time around?

Scott grins a knowing smile.

"There will be a lot more celebrities joining me on the show, over the next few months," he winks.

An (even more) star-studded look at skincare that doesn't cost a pretty penny? Count us in!

DIY Skincare Tips, Courtesy Of Scott McGlynn

With three seasons under his belt, it's safe to say, Scott knows a thing or two about getting (and maintaining) good skin.

And, luckily for us, he's only too happy to share the things he's loved most, over the last few months.

As a bonus, these DIY go-to's are manageable for most.

"I always love creating a fun way to do skincare, but of course, it's all stuff I'm using - so fans can try it all at home. And, it doesn't break the bank!"

So, what does he recommend?

"I did a fresh avocado face mask, which went down very well with viewers," he lists.

That, and his teabag hack for reusable makeup pads.

The key to good skin lies in the kitchen, it seems!

... And The Things Scott Suggests Leaving To The Professionals

Scott may be the king of DIY, but there is a limit, he laughs.

When it comes to the more invasive treatments, he's a strong advocate for not trying it at home.

"I always keep microneedling, face peels, and the like to the professionals in skin clinics, rather than at home, because if something went wrong, you're in the right hands - of people who can look after you."

It's no surprise, then, that Scott isn't a fan of DIY needling videos popping up on his feed.

"It does scare me a bit," he explains. "These things are very intense, and not to be messed with at home."

We'll leave the at-home treatments to avocado masks and teabag makeup removers, then.

After all, if we're taking anyone's advice, it's the guru behind Celebrity Skin Talk!
