John Rainey Obituary, Death – On August 17, 2021, John Rainey of Republic, Missouri, peacefully went home to be with his Lord at the age of 76 years, 4 months, and 8 days. Surrounded by the love of his family, John’s passing marks the end of a life well-lived and the beginning of an eternal journey. Born on March 30, 1945, in Springfield, Missouri, John was the youngest of two children born to Ellis and Minnie (Orr) Rainey. He grew up in the tight-knit community of Republic and graduated from Republic High School, proudly earning his diploma as part of the Class of 1963.

On July 4, 1964, John embarked on a lifelong journey of love and partnership when he married the love of his life, Linda Back. Their union was blessed with the joy of two daughters, Shelly and Christy. Together, John and Linda created a loving home filled with warmth, laughter, and cherished memories. John dedicated 27 years of his life to manufacturing at Dayco, where his hard work and dedication left a lasting impact. His commitment extended beyond the workplace, as he became an active member of the Shriners and the Masons, Lodge #284 in Aurora.

Additionally, John and Linda were longtime members of Northside Christian Church, where they found strength, support, and community. An avid enthusiast of various hobbies, John found joy in fishing, attending drag races in Rogersville, watching NASCAR on television, building hot rods, coin collecting, and tending to the land as a farmer. Yet, above all else, John’s heart belonged to his family. He cherished the moments spent with his wife, daughters, and grandchildren, finding fulfillment in the simple joys of life. John is preceded in death by his parents, Ellis and Minnie Rainey.

He is survived by his devoted wife of 57 years, Linda, his daughters, Shelly Cook and Christy Barnett, and son-in-law Wylie Barnett. John’s legacy lives on through his beloved grandchildren, Kayla Cook, Anna Barnett, Brandon Barnett, and the newest addition to the family, great-grandson Logan Owen Gonzalez. He is also survived by his sister, Gayle Copeland, sister-in-law Darlene Bilyeu, as well as nieces, nephews, and other extended family and friends. As we mourn the loss of a remarkable man, let us celebrate the life of John Rainey, a loving husband, father, grandfather, and friend whose memory will forever remain in the hearts of those who were fortunate enough to share in his journey. May he rest in eternal peace, and may his family find solace in the countless memories they shared with him.
