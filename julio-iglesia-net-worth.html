Early Life

Julio José Iglesias de la Cueva, known simply as Julio Iglesias, was born on September 23, 1943, in Madrid, Spain.

His father, Julio Iglesias Sr., was a medical doctor, and his mother, Maria del Rosario de la Cueva y Perignat, had Jewish ancestry.

Julio initially pursued a career in law and even played professional football as a goalkeeper for Real Madrid Castilla.

However, a car accident that left him unable to walk for two years redirected his focus towards music.

During his recovery, he discovered his musical talent and learned to play the guitar.

“Music gave me a way to express myself and connect with others in a way that words alone couldn’t.” – Julio Iglesias

Entertainment Career

Julio Iglesias began his music career in 1968 after winning the Benidorm International Song Festival. He quickly rose to fame with the release of his debut album, “Yo Canto,” in 1969. Iglesias captivated audiences around the world with his melodic voice and songs sung in multiple languages.

Throughout the 70s and 80s, Julio Iglesias achieved international success with a string of hit songs and albums. His music resonated with listeners from different cultures and backgrounds, reflecting his versatility as an artist. With a discography comprising 80 albums, Iglesias has sold nearly 300 million copies worldwide.

Julio Iglesias has received over 2,600 certified gold and platinum records, a testament to his enduring popularity and wide-reaching appeal.

What sets Iglesias apart is his ability to sing in 14 different languages, connecting with audiences on a global scale. His songs evoke a range of emotions and showcase his impressive vocal range. From heartfelt ballads to upbeat dance tracks, Julio Iglesias has consistently delivered memorable music throughout his career.
